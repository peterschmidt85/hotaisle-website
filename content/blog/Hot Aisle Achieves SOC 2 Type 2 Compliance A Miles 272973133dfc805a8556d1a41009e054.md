# Hot Aisle Achieves SOC 2 Type 2 Compliance: A Milestone in Security and Trust

At Hot Aisle, transparency, trust, and reliability are core to everything we build. Today, we’re proud to announce that we have successfully completed **SOC 2 Type 2 compliance**—a major milestone in our commitment to providing the most secure, dependable, and developer-first cloud experience.

Our journey toward full SOC 2 compliance began with [achieving **SOC 2 Type 1**](Hot%20Aisle%20Achieves%20SOC%202%20Type%201%20Compliance%20224973133dfc80bf9298e97948305239.md), which validated that we had the right security and operational controls designed and in place. Type 1 was an important first step—it proved our commitment to building a secure foundation for customers and set the stage for the Type 2 audit. With that groundwork established, we were able to demonstrate not just that our controls exist, but that we consistently operate them over time—culminating in today’s Type 2 milestone.

### **What This Means**

SOC 2 Type 2 is the standard for demonstrating that a company not only has the right security and operational controls in place but also operates those controls consistently over time. This rigorous, independent audit proves that Hot Aisle meets the highest standards for safeguarding data, ensuring privacy, and maintaining resilience.

### **Why It Matters for Developers and Enterprises**

AI workloads demand uncompromising performance *and* uncompromising trust. By completing SOC 2 Type 2, Hot Aisle offers both:

- **Secure access to AMD MI300x clusters** running on enterprise-grade Dell hardware.
- **Peace of mind** for enterprises and developers who need compliance assurances.
- **Operational excellence** validated by third-party auditors.

### **A Step Forward in Our Mission**

From the beginning, our mission has been to democratize access to advanced compute. With SOC 2 Type 2 certification, we strengthen our ability to serve both the scrappy developer spinning up their first VM and the enterprise running mission-critical AI workloads.

This milestone reflects our belief that building the future of compute requires not just speed and scale—but security, accountability, and trust.
